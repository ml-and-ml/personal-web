About Me

I'm a writer and computational cancer scientist with expertise in computer vision, machine learning, and big data. I leverage computational pathology to better understand the biological mechanisms which inform disease prognosis.

My work lives at the intersection of whole-slide imaging and survival modelling. The slides are enormous — gigabyte-scale gigapixel images — and the clinical signal is sparse, so the methods combine self-supervised representation learning, deep clustering, and end-to-end weakly-supervised training to make prediction tractable from raw tissue alone.

I encourage you to test the models below on your own datasets or expand on them with your own creativity. Feel free to contact me for implementation assistance.
